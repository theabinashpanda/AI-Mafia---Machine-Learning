<h2 class="mp-h2" id="mp-tfa-h2"><span id="From_today.27s_featured_article"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="From_today's_featured_article">From today's featured article</span></h2>
<div id="mp-tfa"><div id="mp-tfa-img" style="float: left; margin: 0.5em 0.9em 0.4em 0em;">
<div class="thumbinner mp-thumb" style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 128px;">
<a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Iggy-Pop_1977.jpg" title="Pop in 1977, the year The Idiot was released"><img alt="Pop in 1977, the year The Idiot was released" data-file-height="800" data-file-width="664" decoding="async" height="154" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/Iggy-Pop_1977.jpg/128px-Iggy-Pop_1977.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/Iggy-Pop_1977.jpg/192px-Iggy-Pop_1977.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/Iggy-Pop_1977.jpg/256px-Iggy-Pop_1977.jpg 2x" width="128"/></a><div class="thumbcaption" style="padding: 0.25em 0; word-wrap: break-word;">Pop in 1977, the year <i>The Idiot</i> was released</div></div>
</div>
<p><i><b><a href="/wiki/The_Idiot_(album)" title="The Idiot (album)">The Idiot</a></b></i> is the debut <a href="/wiki/Album#Studio" title="Album">studio album</a> of American musician <a href="/wiki/Iggy_Pop" title="Iggy Pop">Iggy Pop</a>, released on March 18, 1977, by <a href="/wiki/RCA_Records" title="RCA Records">RCA Records</a>. In 1976 Pop accompanied his friend <a href="/wiki/David_Bowie" title="David Bowie">David Bowie</a> to Europe to rid themselves of their drug addictions. There Bowie agreed to produce an album for Pop. Recording began in June 1976 and further sessions took place at <a href="/wiki/Musicland_Studios" title="Musicland Studios">Musicland Studios</a> in <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a> in August. Bowie composed most of the music and contributed a major portion of the instrumentation, while Pop wrote most of the lyrics. The album's title was taken from <a href="/wiki/Fyodor_Dostoevsky" title="Fyodor Dostoevsky">Fyodor Dostoevsky</a>'s <a href="/wiki/The_Idiot" title="The Idiot">novel of the same name</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Erich_Heckel" title="Erich Heckel">Erich Heckel</a>'s painting <i>Roquairol</i> inspired its artwork. When released the album received divided, albeit largely positive, reviews. It charted in the US, the UK, and Australia. Pop supported <i>The Idiot</i> with a <a href="/wiki/Concert_tour" title="Concert tour">tour</a> in 1977, with Bowie as his keyboardist. <i>The Idiot</i> has continued to be received positively, with many noting Pop's artistic evolution. It has influenced <a href="/wiki/Post-punk" title="Post-punk">post-punk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Industrial_rock" title="Industrial rock">industrial</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Gothic_rock" title="Gothic rock">gothic</a> acts, including <a href="/wiki/Joy_Division" title="Joy Division">Joy Division</a>.  (<b><a href="/wiki/The_Idiot_(album)" title="The Idiot (album)">Full article...</a></b>)
</p>

<p><b><a href="/wiki/Banknotes_of_Demerary_and_Essequibo" title="Banknotes of Demerary and Essequibo">Banknotes of Demerary and Essequibo</a></b> were issued by the British colonies of <a href="/wiki/Demerara" title="Demerara">Demerara</a> and <a href="/wiki/Essequibo_(colony)" title="Essequibo (colony)">Essequibo</a> from 1809 through 1839. They were dual-denominated in Guilders and <i>Joes</i>, a term used by the British colonists to refer to the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_real" title="Portuguese real">Portuguese gold Johannes coin</a> and the notes that eventually replaced them. Despite roughly 30 years of use, the only <i>Joe</i> notes known to exist are unissued remainders from the 1830s; this two-<i>Joe</i> banknote forms part of the <a href="/wiki/National_Numismatic_Collection" title="National Numismatic Collection">National Numismatic Collection</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Smithsonian_Institution" title="Smithsonian Institution">Smithsonian Institution</a>.<br/>
